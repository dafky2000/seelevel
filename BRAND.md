# SeeLevel - Brand

A play on **sea level**: ViewPoint.ca covers coastal Nova Scotia, and the name
itself is the wordplay - *View → See*, *Point → Level* (perception + a
reference line). The product gives a calm, clear read on the market.

## The mark

A sun setting at the sea horizon on a dark badge. The waterline is the
"level" - a reference line - and the filled sea doubles as an analytics area
chart. Master: [`icons/icon.svg`](icons/icon.svg).

## Voice

SeeLevel sounds like a knowledgeable neighbour, not a salesperson.

- **Clear** - plain language, no jargon. Like an uncluttered horizon.
- **On the level** - honest and factual; never overstates. It ships a
  data-source disclaimer rather than hiding one.
- **Calm** - measured and unhurried. Quiet confidence, no hype.
- **Grounded** - unpretentious, East-Coast plainspoken.

**Do:** short sentences, active voice, concrete nouns (*listings*, *trends*,
*zone*), plain verbs (*see*, *draw*, *filter*, *export*), sentence case.

**Don't:** hype words (*powerful*, *unlock*, *supercharge*, *effortless*),
exclamation marks, ALL-CAPS emphasis, jargon, or promises about the user's
outcomes - describe the tool, let the data speak.

| Off-voice | On-voice |
|---|---|
| "Unlock powerful market insights!" | "See price and volume trends as you browse." |
| "No data available." | "No listings yet - browse viewpoint.ca/map to start." |
| "Draw a zone to supercharge your analysis." | "Draw a zone to filter results to one area." |

Tagline: **the Nova Scotia market, on the level.**
